Nutrition basics

Start here. These are the fundamentals that 90% of healthy eating comes down to.

🍽️

How to build a balanced meal

The easiest framework — just picture your plate in sections:

  • ½ plate — fruits & veggies (color + fiber)
  • ¼ plate — protein (keeps you full, builds muscle)
  • ¼ plate — carbs (your body's main energy)
  • Add a thumb of healthy fats & water
🍗

Protein sources

Builds and repairs muscle, keeps you full longest.

  • Chicken, turkey, lean beef
  • Eggs & egg whites
  • Greek yogurt & cottage cheese
  • Fish & canned tuna
  • Beans, lentils, tofu, edamame
🍠

Carb sources

Your main fuel — especially around workouts.

  • Rice, oats, quinoa
  • Potatoes & sweet potatoes
  • Whole-grain bread & pasta
  • Fruit & starchy veggies
  • Beans & lentils (also protein!)
🥑

Healthy fat sources

Supports hormones, brain, and keeps meals satisfying.

  • Avocado & olive oil
  • Nuts, seeds & nut butters
  • Salmon & other fatty fish
  • Cheese & whole eggs
🛒

Grocery staples

Keep these on hand and a good meal is always 10 minutes away.

  • Chicken breast, eggs, ground turkey
  • Greek yogurt, cottage cheese
  • Rice, oats, tortillas
  • Frozen veggies & fruit
  • Canned tuna, beans, peanut butter
💡

Basics, explained simply

  • Calories = energy. Balance them with your goal.
  • No food is "bad." It's about overall pattern.
  • Protein + fiber = the fullness power couple.
  • Consistency > perfection. Always.

Easy meal inspiration

Simple, balanced meals I actually eat. Mix and match the proteins, carbs, and veggies you like.

High protein

Cottage cheese bowl

Cottage cheese + berries + a drizzle of honey + granola. ~25g protein, takes 2 minutes.

Post-workout

Chicken & rice bowl

Chicken breast, rice, kimchi or veggies, and a sauce you love. Balanced, filling, meal-preps great.

5 minutes

Tuna crunch wrap

Canned tuna + Greek yogurt instead of mayo, on a tortilla with spinach. Quick high-protein lunch.

Breakfast

Overnight oats

Oats + milk + Greek yogurt + chia + fruit. Stir at night, grab it in the morning.
