01About

I want healthy eating to feel less like a rulebook and more like something you can actually live with.

I'm a Registered Dietitian-to-be studying nutrition at the University of Houston. I love lifting, bodybuilding, sweet potatoes, yogurt bowls, and meals that make you feel strong — not restricted. My goal is to become the kind of dietitian who helps women fuel their bodies, build muscle, recover well, and understand nutrition without all the fear, extremes, or "what I eat in a day" comparison spirals.

02 The fundamentals

Nutrition basics I come back to

01

Build around the big three

Most meals become easier when you start with:

  • Protein fullness + muscle support
  • Carbs training fuel + energy
  • Fats hormones + satisfaction
02

Carbs are not the enemy

Rice, oats, potatoes, fruit, beans, and bread can all fit. Especially if you lift, carbs are your friend.

03

Protein does not need to be complicated

Chicken, turkey, egg whites, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, tuna, beans, and protein powder when useful — that's enough.

04

Consistency beats perfect tracking

You do not need to make food your full-time job. A few meals you can repeat will take you so far.

05

Eat enough to support your goals

If you want to build, train hard, recover, and feel good, under-eating is not the flex. Fueling is.

06

Make the healthy choice easy

Keep staples ready: chicken, rice, potatoes, yogurt, cottage cheese, fruit, frozen veggies, avocado, oats, and eggs.

03

Protein. Carb. Produce. Add fat. Done.

That is the meal-building method I come back to constantly. It keeps nutrition simple whether you track macros, eyeball portions, meal prep, or throw something together in five minutes.

04Meal inspiration

Meals I actually eat on repeat

Signature plate

Chicken, sweet potato & cottage cheese

Chicken breast tenders, sweet potato, cottage cheese, avocado, steamed veggies, pickled beets, cinnamon, and salt. Simple, filling, and very me.

Sweet treat

Greek yogurt bowl

Greek yogurt, fruit, honey, granola or cereal, cinnamon, and sometimes protein powder. Basically dessert that still fuels me.

Pre-workout

Rice cakes + banana

Caramel rice cakes with banana when I need something easy before training. Fast carbs, zero drama.

Post-workout

Rice bowl

Rice, chicken or turkey, veggies, avocado, kimchi or salsa, and a sauce I actually like. Balanced does not have to be boring.

High-protein snack

Cottage cheese + fruit

Cottage cheese with berries, honey, cinnamon, or granola when I want something quick but still satisfying.

Meal prep staple

Ground turkey + carbs

Ground turkey patties with rice, potatoes, beans, or tortillas. Easy to prep, easy to repeat, easy to build around.

06Good to know

Common questions

Is this medical or personalized nutrition advice?

No — everything here is general educational content. For individualized medical nutrition therapy, work with a registered dietitian one-on-one.

Do I have to count calories or macros?

No. Tracking can be a tool, but it is not the only way to build balanced meals or make progress.

Can I still eat carbs?

Yes. Carbs are your body's main energy source, and they can be especially helpful if you lift, train, or want to feel energized.

Who is this for?

Girls who want to eat balanced, fuel workouts, build muscle, stop fearing food, and make nutrition feel simple again.

Are 1:1 services available?

Not yet. I am still a dietitian-in-training, but the long-term goal is to offer support once I am credentialed.
