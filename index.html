01 About Me

My passion for nutrition grew from my own health and fitness journey.

Today, I'm completing my Human Nutrition & Foods degree while working toward becoming a Registered Dietitian. Through my education and personal experience, I've learned that nutrition is not about perfection. It is about building habits that support your goals, health, performance, and quality of life. When I'm not studying, you can usually find me strength training, creating meals, spending time with family, or finding new ways to make sweet potatoes even better.

02 Why Nutrition

Food is more than calories and macros.

I believe food should help you feel strong, energized, and supported, not overwhelmed or restricted.

My goal is to become the kind of dietitian who helps women understand nutrition in a way that feels practical and empowering. I care about balanced meals, nutrients, fueling workouts, recovery, body composition goals, and developing a healthier relationship with food. I want women to know that they can pursue a strong, confident physique while still nourishing their bodies well.

05 Foods That Fuel Me

Meals and staples I come back to again and again.

Sweet potato favorites

Sweet potatoes, protein, and veggies

Chicken, turkey, steak, salmon, or shrimp paired with sweet potatoes, vegetables, avocado, cottage cheese, and plenty of seasoning.

Yogurt and kefir bowls

Fruit, honey, cinnamon, and crunch

Greek yogurt or kefir bowls with berries, banana, granola, cereal, pumpkin seeds, honey, and cinnamon. Simple, high-protein, and so satisfying.

Power bowls

Rice bowls that actually fill you up

Rice with chicken, turkey, beef, shrimp, or salmon, plus vegetables, avocado, kimchi, salsa, or whatever sauce makes it enjoyable.

Everyday snacks

Snacks that support my goals

Rice cakes with banana or honey, stuffed dates, fruit with cottage cheese, oats, and quick options I can use around training or busy days.

Protein-packed plates

Meals that are simple but not boring

Eggs, egg whites, sardines, shrimp, salmon, chicken, turkey, and cottage cheese paired with carbs, produce, and fats.

My food philosophy

Enough food to feel good

I want my meals to support energy, training, digestion, hormones, recovery, and enjoyment. Food should fuel your life, not control it.

06 Movement

Training should support your health, not punish your body.

01

Strength training

Lifting helps build muscle, confidence, strength, and a body that feels capable. You do not need a perfect split to start.

02

Walking and steps

Daily movement matters. Steps can support your goals without turning exercise into punishment.

03

Rest and recovery

Rest days, sleep, stress management, and enough food are part of the plan. Recovery is not optional.

04

Sample training structure

Three, four, or five training days can all work. The best split is the one you can recover from and repeat consistently.

05

Fueling workouts

Carbs, protein, hydration, and timing can make a big difference in how you feel and perform.

06

Physique goals with nourishment

You can care about your physique and still respect your body. The goal is progress without underfueling or extremes.
